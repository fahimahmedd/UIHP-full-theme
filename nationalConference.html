National Intellectual
Property Conference 2024

Protecting Your Idea University Innovation Hub Program

Introduction:

With the vision of Smart Bangladesh by 2041, the role of Intellectual Property (IP) in Bangladesh stands as a critical element for growth and development. This concept note for the National IP Conference 2024 delves into the significance of IP in both the global arena and the specific context of Bangladesh, providing a detailed examination of the various types of IP—patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets—and their crucial roles in fostering innovation and economic progress as a whole, particularly in the tertiary education system of Bangladesh.

University Innovation Hub Program:

The University Innovation Hub Program in Bangladesh represents a strategic initiative to foster a robust innovation and entrepreneurship ecosystem in the tertiary education system, pivotal for thriving in the dynamically evolving landscape induced by the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR). Spearheaded by the Bangladesh High-tech Park Authority (BHTPA) of ICT Division, the 'University Innovation Hub Program’ is a significant step towards this goal. It involves the establishment of vibrant innovation hubs in ten universities, serving as epicentres for nurturing a culture of innovation, promoting an entrepreneurial mindset, culture of intellectual property protection and fostering a collaborative environment among students, graduates, researchers, and faculty members.

This program, funded by the World Bank, is designed to comprehensively transfer knowledge to universities. It includes launching new programs to be accredited by the University Grants Commission and international bodies. The target audience includes students, graduates, alumni, and faculties aspiring to drive technology-enabled innovation and commercialise these innovations through startup ventures. The program offers a plethora of benefits, such as discovery and access to innovation hub facilities, opportunities for interaction with global and national innovation leaders, access to networks, and support for intellectual property rights (IPR) protection.

Within the broader context of the PRIDE Project under the ICT Division, the University Innovation Hub Program aims to strengthen the digital entrepreneurship and innovation ecosystem in Bangladesh. It is part of the project’s component of ‘Strengthening the Digital Entrepreneurship and Innovation Ecosystem (DEIED)’. The initiative involves establishing incubation centres within selected universities and software technology parks to encourage digital entrepreneurship through integrating incubation, acceleration, and mentorship programs.

IP in the Bangladesh Context: Program:

In Bangladesh, the integration of a robust IP framework is a stepping stone towards achieving a knowledge-based economy. The country's current IP landscape is an intricate mix of emerging policies and practices that are gradually aligning with international standards.

This transition is pivotal for Bangladesh to secure its place in the global economic order. However, challenges such as inadequate enforcement of IP laws, limited public awareness about IP rights, and the need for more specialised education in IP management remain significant hurdles. Thus, Bangladesh ranks 105th among 132 economies on the Global Innovation Index in 20231 and 93rd in the world for patent filings2. There remains significant scope for improvement.

The comparison of Bangladesh’s IP landscape with global benchmarks reveals both gaps and potential areas for improvement. While Bangladesh has made strides in establishing IP laws and participating in international IP agreements, it lags in effective enforcement and integration of IP in key sectors such as technology, pharmaceuticals, and creative industries. To bridge these gaps, Bangladesh needs to enhance its legal frameworks, invest in IP education and awareness, and foster a culture of innovation that respects and protects intellectual creations.

National Conference on IP and Commercialization 2024:Program:

In the above context, as a part of the deliverables of the UIHP, BHTPA in collaboration with BUET is going to organise the first of its kind National IP Conference during the 1st week of March 2024. The conference is expected to be joined by honourable government high officials, members of Bangladesh University Grants Commission (UGC) and high officials from Department of Patents, Designs and Trademarks - DPDT, Bangladesh. It is also expected that officials from WIPO, USPTO and UPM will join the conference.

The National Intellectual Property (IP) Conference will prominently feature honourable Vice- Chancellors, Deans, Heads of Departments, Faculties and students, alongside experts in intellectual property from home and abroad. This gathering is also set to include key figures from the ICT division, Bangladesh Hi-Tech Park Authority (BHTPA) and the World Bank.

Objectives of the conference

  •  Promote IP Awareness: To raise awareness among key stakeholders, including government officials, academia, industry professionals, and the public, about the importance of IP in driving innovation and economic development.
  •  Foster IP Education and Strategy Development: To emphasise the need for specialised education in IP laws and management, and to develop effective strategies for IP management and protection in various sectors.
  •  Knowledge Transfer: Knowledge Transfer on IP system development in university settings.
  •  Awareness: Awareness about IP Commercialization and roles of universities

Outcome of the Conference

  •  Increased Awareness in Academia and Industry: It seeks to raise awareness about IP's importance in universities and industries, emphasising the integration of IP in research and development.
  •  IP Management Strategies: Workshops will offer strategies for managing IP portfolios in academic settings, fostering an IP-aware culture within educational institutions.
  •  Networking and Collaboration: The conference will provide networking opportunities among experts, officials, and industry leaders, encouraging collaborations for future IP- related initiatives
  •  Policy Recommendations: The event is expected to generate policy recommendations for reforms in Bangladesh's IP framework, aiming to align with global standards and boost innovation-driven economic growth.
