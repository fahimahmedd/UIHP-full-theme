Why IC?

The Innovation Cohort is an exciting part of the University Innovation Hub Program tailored just for you – the creative thinkers, the dreamers, and the doers. It's where your ideas get to see the light of day, transforming from mere thoughts into tangible, market-ready innovations. In this hands-on journey, you'll team up with fellow students, faculty, and alumni to explore the full spectrum of bringing an idea to life, from the initial spark of inspiration to the final stages of commercialization. Under the guidance of experienced mentors and through engaging workshops, you'll develop not just a product or service, but also the entrepreneurial mindset and skills crucial for thriving in today's innovation-centric world. This is your playground for innovation, where learning meets doing, and your ideas can flourish and impact the real world. Welcome to the Innovation Cohort – where your entrepreneurial journey begins

Who can Join? (Eligibility)

Team Composition: Applicants must apply as teams, not individually. This fosters a collaborative environment crucial for successful innovation projects.

Team Size and Diversity: Each team should consist of 3-5 members.

Gender Diversity: To ensure inclusivity and promote gender diversity, each team must include at least one female member. This requirement emphasizes the program's commitment to supporting diverse innovation ecosystems.

Affiliation: Teams can be made up of students (including 3rd and final year students), alumni, or faculty members from any of the participating universities, highlighting the program's broad reach within the academic community.

No Prior Experience Required: Importantly, applicants do not need to have prior coding experience. This opens up the program to a wider audience, ensuring that individuals from non-technical backgrounds can also participate and contribute their ideas and skills to innovation projects.

Benefit of the course

  • Collaborative Learning Environment: Dive into a team-based experience where diversity of thought and expertise converge. Working alongside peers from different disciplines, you'll harness the collective power of your diverse backgrounds to tackle challenges and uncover novel solutions.
  • Mentorship from Industry Experts: Receive unparalleled guidance and insights from seasoned mentors who have navigated the path of innovation and entrepreneurship. Their experience is your roadmap, offering personalized advice and critical feedback to steer your project towards success.
  • Hands-on Experience: Transition from theoretical knowledge to practical application. The Innovation Cohort enables you to develop prototypes and Minimum Viable Products (MVPs), providing a tangible manifestation of your innovative ideas and a critical step towards commercialization.
  • Access to Resources and Workshops: Benefit from a wealth of resources, including workshops that cover essential topics like ideation, prototyping, business modeling, and pitching. These sessions are designed to equip you with the tools and knowledge necessary for your entrepreneurial journey.
  • Networking Opportunities: Connect with like-minded individuals, industry professionals, and potential investors. The Innovation Cohort is not just a program; it's a gateway to a vibrant community of innovators and entrepreneurs, offering endless possibilities for collaboration and growth.
  • Foster an Entrepreneurial Mindset: Cultivate resilience, adaptability, and a problem-solving approach—qualities essential for success in today's dynamic world. The Innovation Cohort challenges you to think big, take calculated risks, and embrace failure as a stepping stone to success.
  • Showcase Your Innovation: Gain the opportunity to present your project to a wider audience, including potential investors and industry leaders. This visibility can open doors to funding, partnerships, and invaluable support as you move forward.
  • Empower Your Career: Whether you aspire to launch your own startup or innovate within existing organizations, the skills and experiences gained through the Innovation Cohort will set you apart in the job market, preparing you for a future as a change maker.

Who are the Instructors?

In the dynamic landscape of the Innovation Cohort, part of our University Innovation Hub Program, you'll have the unique opportunity to learn from two distinct types of mentors: Business Mentors and Technical Mentors.

Business Mentors are your navigators in the vast ocean of commercialization. They bring to the table their rich experience in business strategies, financial modeling, marketing tactics, and the nitty-gritty of navigating the marketplace. With them, you'll learn how to turn your innovative ideas into viable business ventures that resonate with your target audience and attract potential investors.

On the flip side, Technical Mentors are the architects and builders of your innovation journey. They possess deep knowledge in product development, from the inception of an idea to the creation of a tangible prototype or a Minimum Viable Product (MVP). These mentors will guide you through design thinking processes, prototyping methodologies, and the technical validation of your ideas, ensuring your innovations can withstand real-world challenges.

Together, these mentors form the backbone of the Innovation Cohort, offering you a balanced education in both the technical prowess and business acumen needed to bring your innovative projects to life. This holistic mentoring approach ensures you're well-equipped to navigate the complexities of entrepreneurship and innovation, making your journey from concept to commercialization both enlightening and successful.

Dive into this journey with us, where your ideas will not only be nurtured but transformed into impactful solutions ready for the world stage.

How to Apply?

What's the Evaluation Method?

Innovation Cohort teams are assessed through a well-defined evaluation process that includes the following key components

Lean Canvas Readiness: This looks at how well your team has developed the Business Model Canvas. It checks if all parts are detailed and researched thoroughly.

Product Development Status: Evaluates the stage your product is in, from the initial idea to being ready for the market. It considers the progress and milestones you've achieved.

USP (Unique Selling Proposition) and CSP (Core Service Proposition): Assesses how clearly and strongly your product's unique benefits and core services are defined compared to competitors.

Target Market Identification: Determines how well you've identified your core customers — the specific group of people your product or service is aimed at.

Unit Economics: Reviews the profitability of each unit sold, ensuring that revenues exceed the costs, which confirms the financial viability of your product.

Brand Positioning: Evaluates your brand's position in the market, ensuring it resonates with customers and stands out from the competition.

Financial Modelling: Looks at your business's financial plan, forecasting future revenues, costs, and the overall economic feasibility.

Pitch Deck Preparation: Assesses how prepared your pitch deck is. This is crucial for attracting investors and partners.

Team Composition: Reviews the diversity and skill set of your team to ensure it's well-rounded and capable of driving the project forward.

Team Member Attendance: Monitors how regularly team members participate in program activities, which is crucial for maintaining momentum towards project goals.

Evaluation Process:

Weekly Evaluations: Your team will be evaluated weekly by mentors, focusing on strengths, weaknesses, and areas for improvement.Segmented Evaluation Sheet: There are five segments in the evaluation sheet for assessments up to the 16th week. Mentors record their observations, providing scores in specific areas.Cumulative Score: The scores and assessments up until the 8th week determine which teams will pitch to the Jury for funding.Jury Process: This includes both the mentorship team's scoring and the Jury's assessment after your presentations.This evaluation process is designed to track and support your progress closely, offering feedback to help your innovative project succeed.
